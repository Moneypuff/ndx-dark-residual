Read-out

Note on an earlier preliminary cut. A first pass on just 20 mega-caps over 2023–2026, with hand-typed report dates, suggested high DPI preceded a weaker next-day move. That did not survive: with authoritative EDGAR dates, AMC/BMO timing, the full 94-name universe and 8 years of history, the mega-cap next-day effect is −0.05 (p≈0.4, i.e. noise), while the durable signal is the positive 1-month relationship shown here. The earlier result was small-sample/single-window fragility.

Method & caveats

  • Signal (no look-ahead): DPI5 / DPI10 = mean daily DPI over the 5 / 10 trading days ending the session before the report — strictly pre-announcement.
  • Outcome: next-day = adjClose(T+1)/adjClose(T)−1; 1-month = adjClose(T+21 sessions)/adjClose(T)−1, where T is the last clean pre-news close. Timing-aware: for after-hours (AMC) reports T is the report day; for before-open (BMO) reports T is the prior session, so the reaction is always the first full session on the news. Split-adjusted.
  • Dates: SEC EDGAR 8-K Item 2.02 filings, matched to each 10-Q/10-K to isolate the quarterly earnings release (dropping delivery/sales/guidance 2.02s). Acceptance timestamp → US/Eastern gives AMC vs BMO. 94 of 100 names covered; 6 foreign filers (ASML, ARM, PDD, CCEP, FER, NBIS/TRI) file 6-K, not 8-K, and are omitted.
  • Direction: a high dark-pool short-volume ratio is conventionally read as market-makers shorting to fill buy orders — i.e. accumulation. The positive DPI→forward-return link is consistent with that reading.
  • Caveats: effect sizes are small (r≈0.05 next-day, ≈0.10 at a month) — a real but modest tilt, not a stand-alone trade. Single index, mostly-bull sample. DPI is a noisy daily series.
