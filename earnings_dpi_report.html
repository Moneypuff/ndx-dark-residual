Method & caveats

  • Signal (no look-ahead): DPI5 / DPI10 = mean of daily per-name DPI over the 5 / 10 trading days ending the day before the report date. Because these names report after the close, that cut-off is strictly pre-announcement.
  • Outcome: next-day = adj-close(T+1)/adj-close(T)−1 where T is the report day; 1-month = adj-close(T+21 sessions)/adj-close(T)−1. Split-adjusted, so NVDA/AVGO/PANW splits don't distort the windows.
  • Earnings dates are hand-curated (no bulk earnings feed was reachable from this environment) and validated against the price tape — known reactions (META +20%, NVDA −8.5%, AVGO +24%…) reproduce to the tenth of a percent.
  • Take it as suggestive, not decisive: the effect size is modest, it lives inside a single 2½-year bull-market regime, DPI is a noisy daily series, and 20 mega-caps ≠ the whole market. The next-day tercile spread is significant (p≈0.02); the 1-month spread is not.
